Annual Percentage Yield

2020-05-10
explanation
113 words 1 min read
Annual percentage yield1 measures the total amount of interest paid on an account based on the interest rate and the frequency of compounding. $$APY=100[(1 + \frac{interest}{principal})^{\frac{365}{days in term}} -1]$$ $$interest = principal[(\frac{APY}{100} + 1)^{\frac{days in term}{365}} -1]$$ So when a bank advertises that a savings account with them will earn 1.50 % APY, that’s the interest rate, frequency of compounding is usually 365 days or 1 year and principal is the amount you will choose to deposit with them.

Principle of least astonishment

2020-05-05
reference
92 words 1 min read
From Wikipedia: Principle of least astonishment (POLA), also called the principle of least surprise states that a component of a system should behave in a way that most users will expect it to behave; the behavior should not astonish or surprise users. Examples: In Windows operating system, when you hit “F1” on keyboard it usually opens the help for that particular program, if you created a program which instead closed the window - it will astonish the users of your program and should be redesigned according to the principle of least astonishment.

High Availability is the property of a system which measures its uptime. You can translate it into uptime or downtime hours per year by multiplying it with the total number of hours in a year (8760).

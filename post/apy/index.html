Annual Percentage Yield

2020-05-10
explanation
113 words 1 min read

Annual percentage yield1 measures the total amount of interest paid on an account based on the interest rate and the frequency of compounding.

$$APY=100[(1 + \frac{interest}{principal})^{\frac{365}{days in term}} -1]$$

$$interest = principal[(\frac{APY}{100} + 1)^{\frac{days in term}{365}} -1]$$

So when a bank advertises that a savings account with them will earn 1.50 % APY, that’s the interest rate, frequency of compounding is usually 365 days or 1 year and principal is the amount you will choose to deposit with them.
